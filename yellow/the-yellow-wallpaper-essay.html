The Yellow Wallpaper: An Analysis of Feminine Oppression

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman crafts a haunting narrative about the psychological struggles of a woman confined to a domestic space. The wallpaper symbolizes societal oppression and mental illness, reflecting the struggle for identity. Through vivid imagery, Gilman critiques mental health treatment and advocates for women's autonomy.

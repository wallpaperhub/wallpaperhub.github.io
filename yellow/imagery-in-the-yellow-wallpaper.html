Imagery in "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Study of Madness

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman employs rich imagery to convey the protagonist's descent into madness. The wallpaper itself symbolizes her imprisonment, depicting chaotic patterns that reflect her mental state. As she fixates on its design, it transforms into a haunting representation of her struggle for identity and freedom.

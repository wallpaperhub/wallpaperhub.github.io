Exploring Jane in "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Journey to Self-Identity

In Charlotte Perkins Gilman's "The Yellow Wallpaper," the protagonist, Jane, is confined to a room as part of a treatment for her postpartum depression. The oppressive environment, symbolized by the yellow wallpaper, leads Jane into a psychological struggle, ultimately provoking her descent into madness as she seeks freedom and self-identity.

Setting the Scene in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

The setting in "The Yellow Wallpaper" plays a crucial role in representing the protagonist's descent into madness. The story unfolds in a secluded mansion, primarily in a room with yellow wallpaper that becomes a symbol of her oppression. The confining environment reflects societal views on women's roles and mental health in the 19th century.

"The Yellow Wallpaper: A Critical Analysis"

"The Yellow Wallpaper" by Charlotte Perkins Gilman is a profound exploration of mental health and the oppression of women in the 19th century. Through the lens of a woman's descent into madness, it critiques the patriarchal society that confines her. The wallpaper symbolizes the barriers to freedom and self-expression, making the essay a compelling study of identity and agency.

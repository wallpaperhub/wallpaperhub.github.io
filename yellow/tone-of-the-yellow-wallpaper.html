The Tone of "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Study of Madness

The tone of "The Yellow Wallpaper" shifts from oppressive and confined to increasingly desperate and chaotic. Initially, the narrator's voice reflects a sense of obligation and conformity to societal norms. As her mental state deteriorates, the tone becomes more frantic and surreal, ultimately expressing profound feelings of entrapment and rebellion.

Exploring Point of View in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman employs a first-person point of view that immerses readers in the protagonist's deteriorating mental state. This perspective allows for a deep exploration of her feelings of entrapment and oppression, effectively conveying the struggles women faced in a patriarchal society during the 19th century.

Analyzing "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Deep Dive

In Charlotte Perkins Gilman's "The Yellow Wallpaper," the narrative explores themes of mental health, gender roles, and the oppressive nature of patriarchal society. The protagonist's descent into madness symbolizes the stifling confinement that women faced, as represented by the wallpaper itself. Through vivid imagery and powerful symbolism, the story critiques societal norms and advocates for women's autonomy.

Understanding John in The Yellow Wallpaper: A Symbol of Oppression

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," John, the narrator's husband, symbolizes the oppressive forces of patriarchal society. He dismisses her concerns about her mental health, imposing a restrictive "rest cure." Through his condescension and control, John embodies the misogyny of the era, ultimately contributing to her psychological decline.

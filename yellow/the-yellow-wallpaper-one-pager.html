The Yellow Wallpaper: A Study of Madness and Feminism

The Yellow Wallpaper is a poignant short story by Charlotte Perkins Gilman that explores themes of mental illness, gender roles, and societal oppression. Through the narrator's descent into madness, symbolized by the wallpaper in her confinement room, the story critiques the treatment of women in the 19th century. It serves as a powerful commentary on the struggles for autonomy and self-expression.

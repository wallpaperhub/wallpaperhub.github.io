Exploring the Tone in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," the tone shifts from a calm and reflective beginning to increasingly intense and frantic as the narrator descends into madness. The oppressive atmosphere highlights her struggle against societal constraints, evoking feelings of frustration, isolation, and desperation. This pervasive tone underscores the themes of mental illness and identity.

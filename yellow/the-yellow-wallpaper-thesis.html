Understanding "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Thesis Exploration

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman explores themes of women's mental health and the oppressive societal norms of the 19th century. The protagonist's descent into madness reflects the struggles against patriarchy and the quest for autonomy, symbolized by her confinement in the wallpapered room. This critique of gender roles remains relevant today.

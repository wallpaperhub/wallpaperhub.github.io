The Conflict in "The Yellow Wallpaper": A Struggle for Identity

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," conflict arises primarily from the narrator's struggle against oppressive societal norms and her own mental illness. Confined to a room, she battles the constraints imposed by her husband and the medical establishment. This internal and external conflict ultimately leads to her tragic descent into madness, symbolized by the wallpaper itself.

The Yellow Wallpaper: Analyzing Mental Health and Oppression

The Yellow Wallpaper, written by Charlotte Perkins Gilman in 1892, serves as a profound exploration of mental health and societal roles of women. It tells the story of a woman confined to a room, illustrating the destructive effect of isolation and the struggle for autonomy. This compelling narrative emphasizes the importance of self-expression and the consequences of oppression.

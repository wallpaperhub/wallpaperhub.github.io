The Power of Personification in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman masterfully employs personification, particularly in the depiction of the wallpaper itself. The wallpaper becomes a living entity, reflecting the narrator's mental turmoil and entrapment. It symbolizes oppressive societal constraints and ultimately channels the narrator's descent into madness, as she projects her struggles onto its chaotic patterns.

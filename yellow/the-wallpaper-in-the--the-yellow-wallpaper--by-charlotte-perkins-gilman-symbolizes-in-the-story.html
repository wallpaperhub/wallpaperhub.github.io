The Symbolism of the Wallpaper in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

The wallpaper in "The Yellow Wallpaper" by Charlotte Perkins Gilman symbolizes the oppression of women and the struggle for identity. As the protagonist descends into madness, the wallpaper transforms from a mere decoration into a representation of her confinement and the complexities of mental health. Its intricate patterns reflect her chaotic thoughts and desire for freedom.

The Symbolism of the Yellow Wallpaper: A Deep Dive

The "yellow wallpaper" in Charlotte Perkins Gilman's story symbolizes the oppression of women in the 19th century. The protagonist's descent into madness reflects her struggle against societal constraints and mental illness. As she becomes obsessed with the wallpaper, it represents her desire for freedom and self-identity amidst confinement.

Unveiling the Irony in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

The irony in "The Yellow Wallpaper" lies in the protagonist's descent into madness, which is fueled by the very treatment meant to cure her. While prescribed rest and isolation are intended to heal her, these actions imprison her creativity and autonomy, leading to her ultimate rebellion against her confinement.

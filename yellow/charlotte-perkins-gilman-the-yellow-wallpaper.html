"The Yellow Wallpaper: A Feminist Perspective on Mental Health"

Charlotte Perkins Gilman's "The Yellow Wallpaper" explores themes of mental health, gender roles, and societal oppression. Through the narrator's descent into madness, the story critiques the oppressive norms of 19th-century society. The symbolism of the wallpaper reflects her struggle for autonomy and identity, making it a profound feminist text.

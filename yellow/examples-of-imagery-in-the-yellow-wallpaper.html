The Power of Imagery in The Yellow Wallpaper

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman uses vivid imagery to convey the narrator's descent into madness. The description of the wallpaper itself—its unsettling patterns and oppressive yellow hue—symbolizes her entrapment. Nature imagery, like the garden outside, contrasts her confinement, evoking longing for freedom.

Decoding the Symbols in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

Charlotte Perkins Gilman's "The Yellow Wallpaper" is rich in symbolism, particularly revolving around the wallpaper itself, which represents the confinement and repression of women's mental health. The narrator's obsession with the patterns reflects her struggle against societal constraints, ultimately symbolizing the fight for autonomy and liberation.

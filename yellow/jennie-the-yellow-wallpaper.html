"Exploring 'The Yellow Wallpaper': A Study of Mental Health and Oppression"

"The Yellow Wallpaper" is a profound short story by Charlotte Perkins Gilman, exploring the mental health struggles of a woman confined to a room by her husband. The protagonist's descent into madness, symbolized by the yellow wallpaper, critiques the oppressive nature of societal norms and the treatment of women's mental health in the 19th century.

A Deep Dive into Diction in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," Charlotte Perkins Gilman employs meticulous diction to evoke the protagonist's descent into madness. The careful choice of words reflects her emotions and provides insight into her oppressive environment. Vivid imagery and repetitive phrases highlight her entrapment, enhancing the story's themes of mental illness and feminism.

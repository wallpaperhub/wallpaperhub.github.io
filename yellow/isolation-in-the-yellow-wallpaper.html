Understanding Isolation in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," isolation serves as a key theme that exacerbates the protagonist's mental decline. Confined to a room and deprived of social interaction, she becomes increasingly obsessed with the wallpaper, reflecting her struggle against societal constraints and the limits of her own mind. This isolation highlights the dangers of enforced solitude.

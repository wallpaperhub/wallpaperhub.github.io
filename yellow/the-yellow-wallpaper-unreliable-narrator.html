The Unreliable Narrator in "The Yellow Wallpaper"

In "The Yellow Wallpaper," the narrator's descent into madness highlights her unreliability. Confined to a room, her perceptions twist reality, leading readers to question her sanity. This ambiguity invites analysis of mental illness, gender roles, and the oppressive forces that distort her truth, ultimately revealing deeper societal critiques.

Buckley v. Valeo: A Landmark Case on Campaign Finance

Buckley v. Valeo is a landmark Supreme Court case from 1976 that addressed campaign finance laws. The Court held that limitations on individual contributions to political campaigns are constitutional, but restrictions on independent expenditures are unconstitutional, citing free speech protections. This decision significantly impacted campaign financing in the U.S.

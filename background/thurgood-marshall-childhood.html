Thurgood Marshall: A Legacy of Justice from Childhood

Thurgood Marshall, born on July 2, 1908, in Baltimore, Maryland, faced racial discrimination from a young age. Growing up, he was encouraged by his mother, who instilled values of education and resilience. His experiences fueled his passion for justice, ultimately leading him to become the first African American Supreme Court Justice.

Understanding the Dystopia of 1984: A Glimpse into Orwell's Warnings

George Orwell's "1984," published in 1949, is a dystopian novel set in a totalitarian regime. It explores themes of surveillance, propaganda, and individualism against a backdrop of post-World War II political strife. The story follows Winston Smith as he navigates a repressive society where "Big Brother" watches every move, illustrating the dangers of extreme government control.

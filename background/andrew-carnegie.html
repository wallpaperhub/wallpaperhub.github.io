Andrew Carnegie: The Steel Tycoon and Philanthropist

Andrew Carnegie was a Scottish-American industrialist and philanthropist, known for leading the expansion of the American steel industry in the late 19th century. Born in 1835, he emigrated to the U.S. as a child and built a fortune through innovative business practices. Carnegie later focused on philanthropy, establishing libraries and educational institutions.

Understanding the History of Dyslexia

Dyslexia, first described in 1887 by German physician Oswald Berkhan, involves difficulties with reading despite average intelligence. Historical views varied, often misattributing it to laziness or lack of effort. The 20th century brought scientific understanding, recognizing it as a neurological condition, leading to improved awareness and educational approaches.

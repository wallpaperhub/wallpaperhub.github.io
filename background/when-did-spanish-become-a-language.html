When Did Spanish Become a Language?

Spanish emerged as a distinct language in the 9th century, evolving from Vulgar Latin spoken in the Iberian Peninsula. It gained recognition as Castilian in the Kingdom of Castile. By the 15th century, Spanish was firmly established and began its global spread during the Age of Exploration, becoming a major world language.

Origin of the Name Mason: Meaning & History

The name "Mason" has origins in the Old French word "masson," meaning "stone worker" or "builder." It was initially a surname for skilled artisans who worked with stone. The name has historical roots in the Middle Ages, associated with the stonemasonry trade, and over time evolved into a popular given name, symbolizing craftsmanship and strength.

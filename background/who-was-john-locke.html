Who Was John Locke? Uncover the Life and Ideas of a Political Pioneer

John Locke (1632-1704) was a prominent English philosopher and political theorist, often considered the father of liberalism. His influential works, such as "Two Treatises of Government," emphasized natural rights, the social contract, and the importance of government as a protector of individual freedoms. Locke's ideas laid the groundwork for modern democracy and human rights.

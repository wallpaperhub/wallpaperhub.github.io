"The Whimsical World of the Mad Hatter's Tea Party"

The Mad Hatter's tea party, from Lewis Carroll's "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland," sits at the heart of whimsical literature. This eccentric gathering features bizarre characters like the Hatter and March Hare, who engage in nonsensical conversations and riddles over tea. Symbolizing absurdity and the passage of time, it's a beloved scene representing the imagination's freedom.

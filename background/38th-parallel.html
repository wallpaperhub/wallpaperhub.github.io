Understanding the 38th Parallel: The Korean Divide

The 38th parallel is a circle of latitude that serves as the dividing line between North and South Korea. Established at the end of World War II in 1945, it became a significant geopolitical boundary during the Korean War. Today, it symbolizes the ongoing tension and division between the two nations, influencing their politics and culture.

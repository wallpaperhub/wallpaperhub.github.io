Understanding the Cold War: A Brief Overview

The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the Soviet Union and the United States from the late 1940s to the early 1990s. Characterized by ideological differences, military rivalries, and espionage, it shaped international relations. Key events included the Cuban Missile Crisis and the Vietnam War, influencing global policies.

Martin Luther's Impact on Christianity

Martin Luther, a 16th-century theologian, initiated the Protestant Reformation by challenging the Catholic Church's practices, particularly the sale of indulgences. He famously posted his 95 Theses in 1517, advocating for faith-based salvation and scriptural authority. Luther's actions led to the formation of Protestant denominations and significant religious change.

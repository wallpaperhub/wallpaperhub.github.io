Unraveling the Legacy of Standard Oil: A Monopoly's Rise and Fall

Standard Oil, founded by John D. Rockefeller in 1870, revolutionized the petroleum industry by creating a monopoly over oil refining and distribution in the U.S. This corporate giant was known for aggressive business tactics and vertical integration, which led to its eventual breakup in 1911 due to antitrust laws.

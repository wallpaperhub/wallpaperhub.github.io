Historical Background of Human Subjects Protection

The historical background of human subjects protection stems from unethical practices in medical research, particularly highlighted by events like the Tuskegee Syphilis Study and Nazi experiments during WWII. In response, regulations such as the Belmont Report emerged, emphasizing informed consent and the respect for persons, beneficence, and justice.

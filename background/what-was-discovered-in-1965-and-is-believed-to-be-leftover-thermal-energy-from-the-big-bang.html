Discovery of Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation in 1965

In 1965, scientists Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson discovered cosmic microwave background radiation (CMB), believed to be the relic thermal energy from the Big Bang. This faint glow pervades the universe, providing crucial evidence for the Big Bang theory and shaping our understanding of cosmic history and the universe's evolution.

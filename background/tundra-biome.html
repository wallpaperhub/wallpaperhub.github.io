Tundra Biome: Nature's Frozen Wonderland

The tundra biome is a cold, treeless region characterized by its frozen subsoil, short growing seasons, and harsh conditions. It is found mainly in the Arctic and Antarctic regions, supporting specially adapted plants and animals. Key features include permafrost, low precipitation, and unique ecosystems that thrive despite extreme weather.

Why the Berlin Wall Was Built: Key Reasons Explained

The Berlin Wall was built in 1961 to prevent East Germans from escaping to West Berlin. Amid rising tensions during the Cold War, it symbolized the division between the communist East and democratic West. The wall aimed to halt the mass exodus, ensuring the stability of East Germany and reinforcing Soviet influence in the region.

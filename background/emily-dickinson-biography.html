Emily Dickinson: A Pioneering Poet of American Literature

Emily Dickinson (1830-1886) was an American poet known for her unique style, introspective themes, and innovative use of punctuation and slant rhyme. Born in Amherst, Massachusetts, she spent most of her life in seclusion, writing nearly 1,800 poems. Though only a handful were published during her lifetime, her work gained prominence posthumously, marking her as a pivotal figure in American literature.

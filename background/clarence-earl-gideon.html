Gideon v. Wainwright: The Fight for Right to Counsel

Clarence Earl Gideon was a landmark figure in American legal history. Convicted in 1961 for a felony, he famously argued for his right to legal counsel when denied one. His case reached the Supreme Court, which ruled that the Sixth Amendment guarantees the right to counsel for all defendants, leading to major changes in the American judicial system.

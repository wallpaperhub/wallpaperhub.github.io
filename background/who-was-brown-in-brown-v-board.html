Who Was Brown in Brown v. Board?

Brown v. Board of Education, decided in 1954, was a landmark Supreme Court case that declared racial segregation in public schools unconstitutional. The case was named after Oliver Brown, whose daughter, Linda Brown, was denied entry to a whites-only school in Topeka, Kansas, highlighting the injustices of segregation.

"Understanding Bumble Bees: Key Pollinators of Our Ecosystem"

Bumble bees, belonging to the Apidae family, are vital pollinators known for their distinctive black and yellow stripes. They thrive in varied habitats worldwide, contributing to ecosystems by pollinating flowers and crops. Unlike honeybees, bumble bees live in smaller colonies and have a unique ability to 'buzz pollinate,' enhancing their effectiveness in pollination.

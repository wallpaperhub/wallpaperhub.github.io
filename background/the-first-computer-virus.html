Uncovering the First Computer Virus: Creeper

The first computer virus, known as the "Creeper System," was created in the early 1970s by Bob Thomas at BBN Technologies. Originally designed as an experiment, Creeper spread across ARPANET, displaying the message, "I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!" This playful prototype laid the groundwork for future malware, highlighting early cybersecurity challenges.

Discovering the Rich History of Boston College

Boston College, founded in 1863, is a private Jesuit university located in Chestnut Hill, Massachusetts. Originally established as a liberal arts college for men, it began admitting women in the 1970s. The institution has grown significantly, known for its commitment to academic excellence, research, and service, deeply rooted in the Jesuit tradition.

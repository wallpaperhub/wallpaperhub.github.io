Sickle Cell Disease: Understanding Its Impact

Sickle cell disease is a genetic blood disorder characterized by abnormal hemoglobin, which causes red blood cells to become rigid and shaped like a sickle or crescent. This leads to various health complications, including pain episodes, anemia, and increased risk of infections. It's inherited and affects millions worldwide.

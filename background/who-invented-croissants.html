Who Invented Croissants? The Delicious History Revealed

While the exact origin of croissants is debated, they are often linked to Austria, where a similar pastry called kipferl has existed since the early 13th century. The croissant's popularity soared in France in the 19th century, credited to Viennese baker Sylvain Claudius Goy, who introduced the pastry to Paris.

Gideon v. Wainwright: Right to Free Legal Counsel Explained

Gideon v. Wainwright (1963) is a landmark Supreme Court case that established the right to free legal counsel for defendants in criminal cases who cannot afford an attorney. This ruling reinforced the Sixth Amendment, ensuring that the right to a fair trial is upheld. It marked a significant advancement in promoting justice and equality in the legal system.

Exploring the Setting of "To Kill a Mockingbird"

Harper Lee's "To Kill a Mockingbird" is set in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, during the 1930s. This deeply segregated Southern community reflects the social injustices and racial tensions of the era. Through the eyes of Scout Finch, the narrative explores themes of morality, empathy, and the loss of innocence amidst profound societal challenges.

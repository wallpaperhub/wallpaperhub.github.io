Discover Old English: A Journey Through Language History

Old English, also known as Anglo-Saxon, is the earliest form of the English language, spoken from the 5th to the 12th centuries. It evolved from Germanic dialects and features unique vocabulary and grammar. Influenced by Latin and Old Norse, Old English laid the foundation for Middle and Modern English, shaping our language today.

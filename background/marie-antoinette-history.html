Marie Antoinette: The Queen Who Sparked a Revolution

Marie Antoinette, born in 1755, was the last Queen of France before the French Revolution. Originally from Austria, she married Louis XVI at 14. Despite her lavish lifestyle and public perception as a symbol of excess, she was a complex figure, navigating political turmoil until her execution in 1793. Her legacy is one of controversy and intrigue.

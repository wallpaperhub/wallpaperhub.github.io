Explore the Surreal World of Franz Kafka

Franz Kafka, born in 1883 in Prague, was a pivotal figure in 20th-century literature. Known for works like "The Metamorphosis" and "The Trial," Kafka's surreal narratives explore themes of alienation, bureaucracy, and existential anxiety. His unique style combines realism with nightmarish elements, leaving a lasting impact on literature.

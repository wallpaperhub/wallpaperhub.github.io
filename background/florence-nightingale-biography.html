Florence Nightingale: Pioneer of Modern Nursing

Florence Nightingale, born in 1820, was a pioneering nurse and social reformer known as the founder of modern nursing. During the Crimean War, she transformed military healthcare and established nursing as a profession for women. Her innovative practices, including sanitation and statistics, drastically reduced mortality rates among soldiers.

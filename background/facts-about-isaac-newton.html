Incredible Facts About Isaac Newton: The Father of Modern Science

Isaac Newton, a key figure in the Scientific Revolution, was born in 1643. He formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, which laid the groundwork for classical mechanics. Newton also made significant contributions to optics and calculus. His seminal work, "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica," revolutionized science.

Southern Colonies: Agriculture, Economy & Culture

The Southern Colonies, comprising Maryland, Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia, were characterized by their agricultural economy, relying heavily on tobacco, rice, and indigo cultivation. The warm climate and fertile soil facilitated plantations, leading to a reliance on enslaved labor. Social structures emphasized wealth and landownership.

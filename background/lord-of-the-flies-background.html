"Exploring the Impact of Lord of the Flies"

"Lord of the Flies," published in 1954 by William Golding, explores the descent into savagery of a group of boys stranded on an uninhabited island. The novel critiques civilization, human nature, and inherent evil, reflecting the post-World War II context in which Golding wrote. Its themes resonate with the struggle between order and chaos.

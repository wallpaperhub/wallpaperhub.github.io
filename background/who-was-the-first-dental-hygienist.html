Who Was the First Dental Hygienist? Discover Irene Newman!

The first dental hygienist was Irene Newman, who began her career in the early 1900s. Trained by Dr. Alfred Fones, a pioneering figure in dentistry, she played a crucial role in establishing dental hygiene as a profession. Newman is credited with promoting preventive oral care and the importance of regular dental check-ups, laying the foundation for modern dental hygiene.

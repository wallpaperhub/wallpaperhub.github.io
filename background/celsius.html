Understanding Celsius: The Temperature Scale Explained

Celsius is a metric unit of temperature measurement, where 0 degrees represents the freezing point of water and 100 degrees its boiling point at standard atmospheric pressure. It is widely used around the world, especially in scientific contexts, forming a crucial part of the Celsius scale, which aids in everyday temperature readings and climate discussions.

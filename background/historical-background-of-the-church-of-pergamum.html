Explore the Historical Significance of the Church of Pergamum

The Church of Pergamum, located in modern-day Turkey, was a significant center of early Christianity in the 1st century. Known for its impressive altar dedicated to Zeus and the library that rivaled Alexandria's, it was also a hub of pagan worship. The Book of Revelation refers to it as a place of great spiritual challenge, highlighting its struggles against idolatry and false teachings.

Understanding the English Bill of Rights in World History

The English Bill of Rights, enacted in 1689, is a pivotal document in world history that established fundamental rights for citizens and restrained the powers of the monarchy. It laid the groundwork for constitutional governance and influenced democratic principles globally, shaping modern legal systems and the protection of individual liberties.

Old School Hip Hop: Roots and Influences

Old school hip hop emerged in the 1970s in the Bronx, blending rhythm and poetry with vibrant beats. Artists like Grandmaster Flash and Afrika Bambaataa pioneered the genre, emphasizing lyrical prowess and social commentary. The culture embraced breakdancing, graffiti, and DJing, establishing hip hop as a powerful voice for marginalized communities.

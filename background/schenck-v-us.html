Understanding Schenck v. US: Landmark Free Speech Case Explained

Schenck v. United States (1919) was a landmark Supreme Court case concerning free speech during wartime. Charles Schenck distributed pamphlets opposing the military draft, leading to his arrest under the Espionage Act. The Court ruled that free speech is not absolute, establishing the "clear and present danger" test for limiting rights during national security threats.

Discover the Georgia Colony: Origins, Growth & Impact

The Georgia colony, established in 1732, was the last of the Thirteen Colonies. Founded by James Oglethorpe, it served as a refuge for debtors and a buffer against Spanish Florida. Its economy relied on agriculture, with crops like rice and indigo. The colony promoted a unique social framework emphasizing equality and charity.

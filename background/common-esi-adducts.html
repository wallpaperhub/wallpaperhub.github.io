Understanding Common ESI Adducts in Mass Spectrometry

Common ESI adducts are important in mass spectrometry for identifying and analyzing compounds. These adducts form when ions interact with solvent molecules, leading to various species like [M+H]+ (protonation), [M+Na]+ (sodium adduction), and [M-H]- (deprotonation). Understanding these adducts is crucial for accurate chemical characterization.

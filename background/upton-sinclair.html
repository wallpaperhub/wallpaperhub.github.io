Discover Upton Sinclair: The Social Reformer Behind 'The Jungle'

Upton Sinclair was a prominent American writer and social reformer, best known for his novel "The Jungle," which exposed the harsh realities of the meatpacking industry in the early 20th century. His work highlighted the need for progressive reforms and played a significant role in the establishment of food safety regulations in the U.S.

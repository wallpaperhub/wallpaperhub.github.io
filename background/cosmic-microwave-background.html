Exploring the Cosmic Microwave Background: Our Universe's Ancient Glow

The cosmic microwave background (CMB) is the afterglow radiation from the Big Bang, permeating the universe. It provides critical insights into the early universe's conditions and evolution. First detected in 1965, the CMB is uniform but contains fluctuations that reveal the seeds of galaxy formation, helping scientists understand cosmic history.

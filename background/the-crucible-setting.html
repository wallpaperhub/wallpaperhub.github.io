Understanding the Setting of "The Crucible"

The setting of "The Crucible" takes place in the Puritan town of Salem, Massachusetts, during the late 17th century. This repressive environment fosters an atmosphere of fear, superstition, and distrust, where accusations of witchcraft lead to widespread hysteria. It's a backdrop that intensifies the moral conflicts faced by the characters.

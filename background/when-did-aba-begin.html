When Did ABA Begin? A Brief History

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) began in the 1960s, rooted in the principles of behaviorism established by B.F. Skinner. Its application, particularly in treating autism, gained momentum in the 1980s, thanks to pioneers like Ivar Lovaas. Today, ABA remains a prominent and effective approach in behavioral therapy and education.

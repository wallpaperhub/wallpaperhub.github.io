Understanding Black History Month: Significance & Celebration

Black History Month, celebrated every February in the U.S., honors African Americans' contributions and history. It originated from "Negro History Week," established in 1926 by historian Carter G. Woodson. The month serves as a reminder of the struggles and achievements of Black individuals, promoting education and cultural pride.

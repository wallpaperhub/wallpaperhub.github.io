Exploring the Historical Context of Frankenstein

Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein," published in 1818, emerged during the Romantic Era, a time of profound social, scientific, and philosophical upheaval. The Industrial Revolution fueled debates on ethics and humanity, while advances in science, like galvanism, ignited imaginations. Shelley's novel explores these themes, questioning the limits of human ambition and the consequences of playing God.

Understanding Tinker v. Des Moines: Student Free Speech Rights

Tinker v. Des Moines (1969) was a landmark Supreme Court case that upheld students' rights to free speech in public schools. The case involved students wearing black armbands to protest the Vietnam War. The Court ruled that students do not "shed their constitutional rights to freedom of speech or expression at the schoolhouse gate," affirming the importance of individual liberties in educational settings.

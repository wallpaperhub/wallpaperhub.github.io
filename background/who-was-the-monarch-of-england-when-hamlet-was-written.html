Monarch of England During the Writing of Hamlet

When Shakespeare wrote "Hamlet" around 1600, Queen Elizabeth I was the monarch of England, reigning from 1558 until her death in 1603. Her reign was marked by a flourish of arts and culture, setting the stage for Shakespeare's masterpieces. Elizabeth’s influence undoubtedly shaped the themes and success of "Hamlet."

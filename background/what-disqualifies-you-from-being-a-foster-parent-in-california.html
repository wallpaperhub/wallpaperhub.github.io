Disqualifications for Foster Parenting in California

In California, certain factors may disqualify individuals from becoming foster parents. These include felony convictions, child abuse or neglect history, and insufficient financial stability. Additionally, those with severe mental health issues or substance abuse problems may be ineligible. Each case is evaluated individually by agencies.

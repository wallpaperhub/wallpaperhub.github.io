Exploring the California Gold Rush: Impact and Legacy

The California Gold Rush began in 1848 when gold was discovered at Sutter's Mill, igniting a massive influx of prospectors and settlers to the West. Over 300,000 people flocked to California, transforming its economy and landscape. The rush also led to the establishment of towns, and railroads, and had lasting impacts on the indigenous population.

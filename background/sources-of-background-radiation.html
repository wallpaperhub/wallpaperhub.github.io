Understanding Sources of Background Radiation

Background radiation comes from natural sources such as cosmic rays from outer space, terrestrial radiation from the Earth's crust, and radon gas emissions from soil and rocks. Additionally, human-made sources like medical procedures, nuclear power plants, and fallout from nuclear tests contribute to the overall exposure. Understanding these sources helps assess risk.

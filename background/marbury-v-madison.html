Understanding Marbury v. Madison: The Case That Shaped Judicial Review

Marbury v. Madison (1803) is a landmark Supreme Court case that established the principle of judicial review in the United States. It arose when William Marbury's commission was not delivered by Secretary of State James Madison. Chief Justice John Marshall ruled that the Court had the authority to declare laws unconstitutional, thus affirming the judiciary's role in American government.

Understanding Gregg v. Georgia: A Landmark Death Penalty Case

Gregg v. Georgia is a landmark Supreme Court case from 1976 that reaffirmed the constitutionality of the death penalty. The decision came after the Court found that Georgia’s revised death penalty statute addressed previous concerns of arbitrary sentencing. This case laid foundational principles for future capital punishment cases in the U.S.

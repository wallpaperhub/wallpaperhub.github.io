Jack Kirby, born Jacob Kurtzberg in 1917, was a groundbreaking American comic book artist and writer. His work in the 1940s to the 1980s, particularly with Marvel Comics, hugely influenced superhero genres. Known for co-creating iconic characters like the Avengers and X-Men, Kirby's dynamic style and storytelling revolutionized comics.

Ingraham v. Wright: Supreme Court Case Overview

Ingraham v. Wright (1977) was a landmark Supreme Court case concerning the corporal punishment of students in public schools. The Court ruled that the Eighth Amendment's prohibition on cruel and unusual punishment did not apply to public school discipline. This decision upheld the authority of schools to administer corporal punishment, shaping the legal landscape of student rights.

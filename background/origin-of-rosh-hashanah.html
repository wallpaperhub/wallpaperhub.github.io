Discovering the Origins of Rosh Hashanah

Rosh Hashanah, the Jewish New Year, has its origins in the Torah, specifically Leviticus 23:23-25. Observed on the first two days of Tishrei, it marks a time of reflection, prayer, and renewal. Traditionally, it is celebrated with family, sweet foods, and the sounding of the shofar, symbolizing both a call to repentance and a new beginning.

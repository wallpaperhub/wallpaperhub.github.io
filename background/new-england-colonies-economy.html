"Exploring the Economy of New England Colonies"

The economy of the New England colonies was characterized by small-scale farming, fishing, shipbuilding, and trade. The rocky soil and harsh climate limited agriculture, leading to a focus on crops like corn and potatoes. Maritime activities provided wealth, with fishing and whaling being significant contributors to the economy.

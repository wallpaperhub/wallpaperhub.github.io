Summary of Escobedo v. Illinois: A Landmark Case

Escobedo v. Illinois (1964) is a landmark Supreme Court case addressing the right to counsel. Danny Escobedo was denied access to his attorney during interrogation, leading to a confession. The Court ruled that this violated his Sixth Amendment rights, establishing the precedent that defendants must have legal representation during critical stages of criminal proceedings.

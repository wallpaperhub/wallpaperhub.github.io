Why the Civil War Was Fought: Key Reasons Explained

The Civil War was fought primarily over the issues of slavery and states' rights. The Southern states wanted to maintain the institution of slavery, believing it was essential to their economy and way of life, while the Northern states sought to preserve the Union and promote abolition. Tensions escalated, leading to conflict.

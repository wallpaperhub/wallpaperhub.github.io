What Triggered World War I: The Key Events Explained

World War I was ignited by a complex interplay of political alliances, nationalism, and militarism. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in 1914 served as the spark that lit the fuse, leading to a cascade of declarations of war among major powers, ultimately plunging the world into a devastating conflict.

Katherine Johnson: Pioneering Mathematician and NASA Icon

Katherine Johnson was an African American mathematician whose calculations were critical to the success of NASA's early space missions. Born in 1918, she broke through barriers of race and gender, playing a key role in the Apollo moon landing and contributing to the Space Shuttle and Earth Resources satellite programs. Her legacy inspires future generations.

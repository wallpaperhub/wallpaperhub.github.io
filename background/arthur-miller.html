Discovering Arthur Miller: Iconic American Playwright

Arthur Miller was a prominent American playwright known for his works exploring themes of morality, identity, and the American Dream. Born in 1915, his most celebrated plays include "Death of a Salesman" and "The Crucible," both acclaimed for their deep social commentary and rich character development. Miller's influence extends beyond theater, reflecting societal issues of his time.

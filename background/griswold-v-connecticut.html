Understanding Griswold v. Connecticut: Privacy Rights Explained

Griswold v. Connecticut (1965) was a landmark Supreme Court case that established a constitutional right to privacy. The case arose when the state's ban on contraceptives was challenged by Estelle Griswold, the executive director of Planned Parenthood. The Court ruled that the law violated the marital privacy rights, influencing future cases on personal autonomy.

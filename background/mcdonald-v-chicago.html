McDonald v. Chicago: A Landmark Gun Rights Case Explained

McDonald v. Chicago was a landmark Supreme Court case in 2010 that dealt with the application of the Second Amendment to the states through the Fourteenth Amendment. The decision affirmed that the right to bear arms is a fundamental right, thereby striking down Chicago's handgun ban and setting a precedent for future gun rights cases.

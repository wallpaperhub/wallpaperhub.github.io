Explore the Allegorical Depths of Animal Farm

Animal Farm, written by George Orwell in 1945, is an allegorical novella that critiques totalitarian regimes and explores themes of power and corruption. Set on a farm where animals revolt against human owners, it serves as a powerful metaphor for the Russian Revolution and the rise of Soviet communism, illustrating how idealism can lead to tyranny.

Elizabeth Proctor: A Pillar of Strength in The Crucible

Elizabeth Proctor is a central character in Arthur Miller's play "The Crucible," set during the Salem witch trials. As the wife of John Proctor, she embodies strength, resilience, and moral integrity. Elizabeth faces accusations of witchcraft and demonstrates unwavering loyalty to her husband, highlighting themes of love, sacrifice, and justice.

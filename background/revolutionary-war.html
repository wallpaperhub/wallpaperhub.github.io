Understanding the Revolutionary War: Key Facts and Figures

The Revolutionary War (1775-1783) was a pivotal conflict in which the thirteen American colonies fought against British rule. Catalyzed by issues like taxation without representation, it ultimately led to the establishment of the United States. Key figures like George Washington emerged, influencing the nation's identity and governance.

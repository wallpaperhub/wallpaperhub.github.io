Understanding the Causes of the Great Depression

The Great Depression was primarily caused by the 1929 stock market crash, which led to massive bank failures and skyrocketing unemployment. Other factors included overproduction, declining consumer spending, high tariffs like the Smoot-Hawley Tariff, and a lack of international trade. These elements created an economic spiral that deeply affected the global economy.

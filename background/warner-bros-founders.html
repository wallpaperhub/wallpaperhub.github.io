Founders of Warner Bros: The Legacy of the Warner Brothers

Warner Bros was founded by four brothers: Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner in 1923. Initially involved in film distribution, they shifted focus to producing motion pictures, revolutionizing the industry with innovations like synchronized sound in "The Jazz Singer." Their legacy is a cornerstone of Hollywood, creating iconic films and characters.

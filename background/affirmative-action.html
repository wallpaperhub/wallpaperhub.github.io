Understanding Affirmative Action: A Brief Overview

Affirmative action refers to policies designed to increase opportunities for historically marginalized groups, aiming to counteract systemic discrimination. It promotes diversity in education and employment, fostering an inclusive society. Supporters argue it helps level the playing field, while critics question its fairness. Dialogues continue about its impact and effectiveness.

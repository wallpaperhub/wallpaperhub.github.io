Understanding the Historical Background of Galatians

The Epistle to the Galatians, written by the Apostle Paul around 49-55 AD, addresses early Christian communities in Galatia. Paul defends his apostleship and the gospel's purity against Judaizers, emphasizing faith over law. This letter reflects early Church tensions and the struggle for defining Christian identity within a diverse cultural landscape.

"Discovering the Mallard Duck: Facts and Features"

The Mallard duck (Anas platyrhynchos) is a prevalent and distinctive waterfowl commonly found in wetlands across the Northern Hemisphere. Recognizable by its vibrant green head, orange bill, and distinctive quacking, the male is particularly striking while females feature cryptic brown plumage. These adaptable birds thrive in diverse habitats and are known for their migratory behaviors, seasonal variations, and social structures.

"Martin Luther King Jr.: A Legacy of Nonviolent Change"

Martin Luther King Jr. was a pivotal leader in the American civil rights movement, known for his advocacy of nonviolent resistance. Born on January 15, 1929, he inspired millions through his powerful speeches, including the iconic "I Have a Dream." His efforts led to significant social change, promoting equality and justice, ultimately culminating in the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

James Madison: Founding Father & Fourth U.S. President

James Madison, born on March 16, 1751, was the fourth President of the United States and key architect of the Constitution. A prominent Federalist, he played a vital role in drafting the Bill of Rights. Madison's political legacy includes his influence in establishing a balanced government and defending individual liberties. He passed away on June 28, 1836.

Kroger: A Brief History of America's Grocery Giant

Kroger was founded in 1883 by Bernard Kroger in Cincinnati, Ohio. Starting as a small grocery store, it quickly grew, pioneering cash-and-carry retail and customer service innovations. By the 20th century, Kroger expanded through acquisitions, establishing itself as one of the largest supermarket chains in the U.S., providing diverse products and services.

Discover the Origin of Crawfish Boils

Crawfish boils originated in Southern Louisiana, deeply rooted in Cajun and Creole cultures. This communal seafood feast celebrates the harvest of mudbugs from nearby waters, traditionally held in spring. Over time, this festive gathering has spread beyond its origins, becoming a beloved culinary experience enjoyed across the Gulf Coast.

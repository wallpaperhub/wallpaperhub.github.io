Discover A. Philip Randolph: Civil Rights & Labor Leader

A. Philip Randolph was a prominent civil rights leader and labor organizer, best known for his efforts to advance the rights of African American workers. He founded the Brotherhood of Sleeping Car Porters, the first predominantly Black labor union, and played a crucial role in the 1963 March on Washington, advocating for racial equality and economic justice.

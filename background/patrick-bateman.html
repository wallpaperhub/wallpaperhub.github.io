Exploring the Dark Side of Patrick Bateman

Patrick Bateman, the chilling protagonist from Bret Easton Ellis's "American Psycho," embodies the dark side of 1980s high society. A wealthy New York City investment banker, he leads a double life, indulging in extreme consumerism and horrific violence. Bateman's character critiques materialism and social disconnection, leaving a lasting impact on pop culture.

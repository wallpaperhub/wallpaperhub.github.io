History of Cystic Fibrosis: Key Milestones

Cystic fibrosis (CF) was first identified in the 1930s when doctors noticed a correlation between the disease and salty sweat in children. In the 1960s, genetic research pinpointed CF to a mutation in the CFTR gene. Advances in treatment have significantly improved life expectancy, transforming CF from a fatal childhood illness to a manageable chronic condition.

"Understanding the Context of the Letter from Birmingham Jail"

"Letter from Birmingham Jail," penned by Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in 1963, emerged from his imprisonment during a nonviolent protest against racial injustice. Written to address criticisms from white clergymen, King articulates the moral imperative for civil disobedience, emphasizing urgency in the fight for equality and justice.

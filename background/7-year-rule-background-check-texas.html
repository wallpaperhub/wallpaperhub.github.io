The 7-Year Rule for Background Checks in Texas: What You Need to Know

In Texas, the "7-year rule" refers to the limit on reporting certain types of criminal background information. Under this rule, employers can only consider criminal convictions that occurred within the last seven years during background checks. This helps individuals with older offenses have better job prospects.

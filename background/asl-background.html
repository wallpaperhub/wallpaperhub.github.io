Understanding ASL: A Brief Background and Significance

American Sign Language (ASL) has a rich and diverse background that stems from the early 19th century when it was developed by the deaf communities in the United States. It incorporates influences from French Sign Language and local sign dialects. ASL uses hand shapes, facial expressions, and body movement to convey meaning, making it a vibrant visual language.

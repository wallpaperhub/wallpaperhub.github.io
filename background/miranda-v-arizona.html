Understanding Miranda v. Arizona: The Landmark Supreme Court Case

Miranda v. Arizona (1966) is a landmark Supreme Court case that established mandatory warnings for individuals in police custody. Ernesto Miranda's confession, obtained without being informed of his right to remain silent or have an attorney, was deemed inadmissible. This case paved the way for the "Miranda Rights," protecting suspects' rights during interrogation.

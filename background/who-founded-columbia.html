Who Founded Columbia University? Origins & History Explained

Columbia University, founded in 1754, was initially known as King's College. It was established by royal charter in New York City, with the support of the Church of England and some prominent figures of the time, including King George II. The university has evolved significantly, becoming an Ivy League institution recognized for its academic excellence.

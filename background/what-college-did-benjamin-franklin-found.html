What College Did Benjamin Franklin Establish? Discover the University of Pennsylvania

Benjamin Franklin founded the University of Pennsylvania in 1740. Initially established as a charity school, it evolved into one of the first institutions of higher education in the United States. Franklin envisioned a school that would blend practical education with liberal arts, promoting a well-rounded knowledge base for students.

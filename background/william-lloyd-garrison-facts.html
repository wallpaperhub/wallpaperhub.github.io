10 Fascinating Facts About William Lloyd Garrison

William Lloyd Garrison was a leading abolitionist, known for his newspaper, "The Liberator," which called for the immediate end of slavery. Born in 1805, he co-founded the American Anti-Slavery Society in 1833. Garrison advocated for equal rights for women and believed in nonviolent activism. His efforts greatly influenced the abolitionist movement.

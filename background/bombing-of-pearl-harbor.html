Pearl Harbor Bombing: A Turning Point in WWII

The bombing of Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941, was a surprise military attack by the Japanese Navy Air Service against the United States naval base in Hawaii. This devastating assault resulted in significant loss of life and prompted the U.S. to formally enter World War II, marking a turning point in the conflict.

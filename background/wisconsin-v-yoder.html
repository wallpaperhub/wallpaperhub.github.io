Wisconsin v. Yoder: A Landmark Case in Educational Freedom

Wisconsin v. Yoder was a landmark Supreme Court case in 1972 that protected the rights of Amish parents to refuse compulsory education for their children beyond the eighth grade, based on their religious beliefs. This case emphasized the importance of religious freedom, balancing state interests with individual rights.

Understanding Baker v. Carr: A Landmark Redistricting Case

Baker v. Carr (1962) was a landmark Supreme Court case that addressed the issue of legislative redistricting and representation. The Court ruled that redistricting issues could be challenged in federal court, reinforcing the principle of "one person, one vote." This decision marked a significant step towards equitable electoral representation in the United States.

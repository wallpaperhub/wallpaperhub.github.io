The Significance of Matzah: A Closer Look

Matzah, an essential element of the Jewish Passover, symbolizes liberation and the hastiness of the Exodus from Egypt. Traditionally unleavened bread, it commemorates the Israelites' quick departure, leaving no time for dough to rise. Matzah holds deep cultural significance and is central to the Seder meal, fostering reflection and celebration.

Reviving Michigan Central: Detroit's Historic Train Station

Michigan Central, located in Detroit, is a historic train station that exemplifies the city’s rich transportation history. Once the tallest train station in the Western Hemisphere, it fell into disrepair after its closure in 1988. Recently, revitalization efforts have transformed it into a focal point of urban renewal, highlighting cultural and economic growth.

Exploring the Evolution of R&B: From Roots to Modern Influences

The history of R&B dates back to the 1940s, blending jazz, blues, and gospel influences. It gained mainstream popularity in the 1950s with artists like Ray Charles and Sam Cooke. The genre evolved through the decades, giving rise to Motown in the 60s and continuing to shape modern music with stars like Whitney Houston and Usher. R&B remains influential today.

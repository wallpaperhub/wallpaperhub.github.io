Can a President Run After a 4-Year Break?

Yes, a president can run again after a four-year break. The U.S. Constitution allows any person who has served two terms to run again after a hiatus. This means if a former president decides not to seek re-election for a term or takes a break, they may still return and seek the presidency again in the future.

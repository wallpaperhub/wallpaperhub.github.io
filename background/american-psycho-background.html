"Exploring the Dark World of American Psycho"

"American Psycho," a novel by Bret Easton Ellis, was published in 1991, set in the consumer-driven 1980s. It follows Patrick Bateman, a wealthy Wall Street executive and closet psychopath. The story delves into themes of identity, materialism, and moral emptiness, with graphic depictions of violence and satire of yuppie culture.

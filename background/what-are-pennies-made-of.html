What Are Pennies Made Of? Uncovering the Composition

Pennies, which are commonly used in the United States, are primarily made of copper plating over a zinc core. Since 1982, the composition has changed to 97.5% zinc and 2.5% copper. While they may be small in size, these coins have intriguing manufacturing processes and materials that reflect their historical evolution and value.

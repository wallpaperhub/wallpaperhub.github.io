Santa Fe ISD v. Doe: Student Prayer Case Explained

Santa Fe Independent School District v. Doe is a landmark Supreme Court case that addressed the constitutionality of student-led prayer at public school events. In 2000, the Court ruled that such practices violated the Establishment Clause, emphasizing the need for separation of church and state in educational settings. This decision impacts discussions on religious expression in schools.

The Evolution of Bachata Dance: A Cultural Journey

Bachata dance originated in the Dominican Republic during the early 20th century, evolving from rural folk music influenced by African and Spanish cultures. Initially associated with lower social classes, it gained popularity in the 1980s and now thrives globally. Bachata is characterized by its romantic themes and distinct rhythm, often performed in pairs.

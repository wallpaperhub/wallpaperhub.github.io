Horace Mann: The Father of Public Education

Horace Mann, an American educational reformer, championed public education in the 19th century. He believed in free, universal schooling and emphasized the importance of trained teachers. Mann's reforms laid the foundation for the modern education system, advocating for civic responsibility, moral instruction, and equal access to education for all children.

What is Ash Wednesday? A Guide to Its Meaning and Practices

Ash Wednesday marks the beginning of Lent in the Christian calendar, celebrated 46 days before Easter. On this day, many attend church services where ashes, often made from burnt palms, are applied to foreheads in the shape of a cross. This symbolizes repentance, mortality, and the call to reflect on one's life and faith.

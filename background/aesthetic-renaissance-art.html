Aesthetic Renaissance Art: Beauty in Humanism

Aesthetic Renaissance art flourished in the 14th to 17th centuries, emphasizing beauty, harmony, and humanism. It emerged from a renewed interest in classical antiquity, inspiring masterpieces from renowned artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo. This period marked a profound shift in artistic expression, celebrating the human experience and the natural world.

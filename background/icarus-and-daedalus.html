Icarus & Daedalus: The Myth of Flight and Hubris

In Greek mythology, Icarus and Daedalus are renowned figures embodying ambition and hubris. Daedalus, a master craftsman, crafted wings made of feathers and wax for his son Icarus to escape from Crete. Ignoring his father's warnings, Icarus flew too close to the sun, melting the wax, leading to his tragic fall into the sea.

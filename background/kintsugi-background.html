Kintsugi: The Art of Embracing Imperfection

Kintsugi, a traditional Japanese art form, embraces the beauty of imperfection by repairing broken pottery with gold lacquer. This practice symbolizes resilience, highlighting that flaws and scars can add value and character. Originating from the 15th century, kintsugi promotes mindfulness and appreciation for the history of objects, making it a unique fusion of aesthetics and philosophy.

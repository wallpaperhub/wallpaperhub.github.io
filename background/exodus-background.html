Understanding the Exodus: A Historical Overview

The term "exodus" typically refers to a mass departure of people from a particular place, often in search of safety or better living conditions. Historically, it is most notably associated with the biblical account of the Israelites fleeing slavery in Egypt, led by Moses. This event emphasizes themes of liberation, faith, and the quest for a promised land.

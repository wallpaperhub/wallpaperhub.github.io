KFC: A Brief History of the Iconic Fried Chicken Brand

KFC, or Kentucky Fried Chicken, was founded by Colonel Harland Sanders in 1952. Sanders developed his secret blend of 11 herbs and spices to create a unique fried chicken recipe. The first franchise opened in Salt Lake City, Utah, and KFC quickly became popular for its distinctive taste, expanding globally to become a fast-food icon.

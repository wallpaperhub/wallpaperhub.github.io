Understanding Gibbons v. Ogden: Impact on Interstate Commerce

Gibbons v. Ogden (1824) is a landmark Supreme Court case that clarified interstate commerce regulation under the Constitution. The decision strengthened federal power by affirming that only Congress could regulate interstate trade, overriding state laws. This case set crucial precedents for federal authority and economic policy in the U.S.

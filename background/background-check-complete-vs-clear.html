“Background Check: Complete vs. Clear Explained”

Understanding the difference between a "background check complete" and "background check clear" is crucial for job applicants and employers. A complete check means the process has finished, but a clear status indicates no issues were found. Knowing this distinction helps manage expectations during hiring processes.

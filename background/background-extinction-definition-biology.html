Understanding Background Extinction in Biology

Background extinction refers to the normal rate at which species become extinct due to natural processes, such as environmental changes and biological factors. Unlike mass extinctions, which occur suddenly and affect many species, background extinction is gradual and constant, representing the ongoing loss of biodiversity over time.

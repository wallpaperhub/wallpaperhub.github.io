Myths About the Cosmic Microwave Background Debunked

The cosmic microwave background (CMB) is the afterglow of the Big Bang, but it's a common misconception that it is uniform in all directions. While it is largely homogeneous, slight temperature fluctuations indicate the early universe's density variations, which eventually formed galaxies. Thus, saying the CMB is completely uniform is not true.

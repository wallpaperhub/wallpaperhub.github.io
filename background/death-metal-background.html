Everything You Need to Know About Death Metal

Death metal emerged in the mid-1980s as a heavier subgenre of metal, combining the aggression of thrash with elements of doom and black metal. Characterized by growled vocals, fast guitar riffs, and intricate solos, it conveys themes of darkness, tragedy, and violence. Pioneers like Death and Morbid Angel shaped its foundational sound.

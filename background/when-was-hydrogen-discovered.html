When Was Hydrogen Discovered? Key Historical Insights

Hydrogen was first recognized as a distinct element in 1766 by British scientist Henry Cavendish. During experiments, he discovered a gas that produced water when ignited, leading to the identification of hydrogen. Its name, derived from the Greek words for "water" and "creator," emphasizes its vital role in forming water.

Discovering Jay Gatsby: The Tragic American Dream

Jay Gatsby, a central character in F. Scott Fitzgerald's "The Great Gatsby," embodies the American Dream's allure and tragedy. His extravagant parties and opulent lifestyle mask a profound loneliness and unfulfilled love for Daisy Buchanan. Gatsby's relentless pursuit of his dreams ultimately leads to his downfall, highlighting themes of ambition, wealth, and disillusionment.

Ares: The Greek God of War Explained

Ares, the Greek god of war, represents the chaotic and brutal aspects of battle. Known for his fierce and aggressive nature, Ares embodies the violence of conflict, contrasting with Athena's strategic approach to warfare. Often depicted with weapons and accompanied by fear and terror, Ares symbolizes the darker side of human nature. His tumultuous relationships, especially with Aphrodite, further highlight his complex character in mythology.

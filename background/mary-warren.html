Explore Mary Warren: A Crucial Figure in "The Crucible"

Mary Warren is a pivotal character in Arthur Miller's play "The Crucible." As a servant in the Proctor household and one of the accusers in the Salem witch trials, her character embodies the themes of fear, power, and manipulation. Torn between her fear of the court and her loyalty to Abigail Williams, Mary struggles with her conscience, ultimately highlighting the hysteria and moral dilemmas of the time.

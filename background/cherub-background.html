Cherub Background: The Symbol of Innocence

Cherubs, often depicted as chubby, winged infants or young boys, symbolize innocence and divine love in art and literature. Their origins trace back to ancient Mesopotamian and later Christian iconography, where they represent messengers of God. Frequently featured in paintings, sculptures, and religious decorations, cherubs embody purity and grace.

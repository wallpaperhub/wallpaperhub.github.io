Tupac Shakur: The Legacy of Hip-Hop’s Icon

Tupac Shakur, also known as 2Pac, was an influential American rapper and cultural icon. Born on June 16, 1971, in East Harlem, his potent lyrics tackled issues like racial inequality, violence, and poverty. His untimely death at 25 has left a lasting legacy in music and activism, making him a symbol of resilience and creativity.

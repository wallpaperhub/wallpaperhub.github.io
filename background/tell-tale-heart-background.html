"Unraveling the Madness: The Tell-Tale Heart"

Edgar Allan Poe's "The Tell-Tale Heart," written in 1843, explores themes of madness and guilt. The narrator, an unreliable and disturbed individual, insists on sanity while recounting the chilling murder of an old man. The story highlights psychological depth, using vivid imagery and suspense to captivate readers about the human psyche.

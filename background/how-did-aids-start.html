Understanding the Origins of AIDS: How It All Began

AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) is caused by the HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) that likely originated from non-human primates in Central Africa. The virus crossed into humans during hunting and butchering of animals, beginning its establishment in the 20th century. The epidemic rapidly spread through sexual contact, blood transfusions, and intravenous drug use.

Discover the Spectrum: A Guide to Noise Colors

Different types of noise colors, such as white, pink, and brown, are characterized by their frequency distributions. White noise features equal intensity across all frequencies, making it ideal for masking unwanted sounds. Pink noise, with power decreasing as frequency increases, creates a balanced auditory experience. Brown noise, even deeper, emphasizes lower frequencies and provides a soothing effect. Understanding these colors can enhance sound design, relaxation, and sleep quality.

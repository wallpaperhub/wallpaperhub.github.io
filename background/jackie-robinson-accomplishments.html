Jackie Robinson: Trailblazer and Icon of Equality

Jackie Robinson broke baseball's color barrier in 1947, becoming the first African American player in Major League Baseball (MLB). He won the 1947 Rookie of the Year award, was a six-time All-Star, and in 1949 earned the National League MVP. Robinson's impact transcended sports, as he became a civil rights icon advocating for equality.

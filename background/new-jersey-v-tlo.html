Understanding New Jersey v. T.L.O.: Student Rights Explained

In the landmark case New Jersey v. T.L.O. (1985), the U.S. Supreme Court addressed the balance between student rights and school authority. The case involved a student caught smoking in the bathroom, leading to a search of her purse that uncovered drugs. The Court ruled that school officials could conduct searches based on "reasonable suspicion" rather than "probable cause," setting a precedent for students' privacy rights in schools.

John Rolfe and Pocahontas: A Historic Marriage

John Rolfe, an English settler, famously married Pocahontas, the daughter of Powhatan, an Indigenous chief. Their union in 1614 was not only a personal bond but also a strategic alliance that helped foster peace between English settlers and Native Americans. Together, they had a son, Thomas, and their marriage symbolized cultural exchange.

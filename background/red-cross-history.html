Red Cross History: A Legacy of Humanitarian Aid

The Red Cross was founded in 1863 by Henry Dunant and Gustave Moynier, inspired by the horrors witnessed during the Battle of Solferino. It aimed to provide aid to wounded soldiers and established the principles of humanitarianism. Over the years, it evolved into a global network, responding to crises and promoting health and safety worldwide.

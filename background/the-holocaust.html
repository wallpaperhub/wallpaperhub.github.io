The Holocaust: Remembering the Victims and Lessons Learned

The Holocaust, a devastating genocide during World War II, claimed six million Jewish lives, along with millions of others, including Romani, disabled, and Polish individuals. Initiated by Nazi Germany, it was a horrific example of anti-Semitism and hatred. Remembering the Holocaust is crucial to prevent future atrocities and uphold human rights.

Hazelwood v. Kuhlmeier: A Landmark Student Speech Case

The Hazelwood v. Kuhlmeier case (1988) centered on a high school newspaper's censorship. The Supreme Court ruled that schools could limit student expression in school-sponsored activities, emphasizing the need for educational settings to promote a certain standard. This landmark decision balanced student rights against educational objectives.

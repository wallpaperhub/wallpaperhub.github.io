Dred Scott Decision: A Pivotal Moment in U.S. History

The Dred Scott Decision, rendered by the U.S. Supreme Court in 1857, declared that African Americans, whether free or enslaved, could not be American citizens. The case heightened tensions over slavery, asserting that Congress lacked the power to regulate slavery in the territories. This pivotal ruling deepened divisions and foreshadowed the Civil War.

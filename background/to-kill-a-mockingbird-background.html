To Kill a Mockingbird: A Classic Tale of Innocence and Justice

Harper Lee's "To Kill a Mockingbird," published in 1960, is set in the racially charged Deep South during the 1930s. The novel explores themes of morality, social injustice, and childhood innocence through the eyes of Scout Finch, a young girl. It reflects the struggles of a community grappling with prejudice and moral integrity.

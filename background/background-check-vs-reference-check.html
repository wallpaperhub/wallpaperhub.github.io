Background Check vs Reference Check: Key Differences Explained

Background checks and reference checks are both essential in the hiring process but serve different purposes. A background check verifies a candidate's criminal history, credit status, and work experience, ensuring a safe and qualified hire. Conversely, reference checks involve contacting former employers or colleagues to gauge a candidate's work ethic, skills, and character, providing insight into their suitability for the position.

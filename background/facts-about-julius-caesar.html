Fascinating Facts About Julius Caesar

Julius Caesar, a pivotal figure in Rome's history, was born in 100 BC. He expanded the Roman Republic through conquests, notably Gaul. Caesar declared himself dictator for life but was assassinated on the Ides of March, 44 BC. His legacy includes significant reforms and the transition from Republic to Imperial Rome.

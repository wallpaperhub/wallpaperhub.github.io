Understanding Holden Caulfield's Complex Personality

Holden Caulfield, the protagonist of J.D. Salinger's "The Catcher in the Rye," is characterized by his deep-seated cynicism, alienation, and emotional turmoil. He embodies teenage rebellion, often criticizing the adult world's phoniness. Despite his bitterness, he seeks connection and understanding, showcasing vulnerability beneath his tough exterior.

Discover the Fascinating History of Vaccines

Vaccination dates back to ancient times, with early practices observed in China and India. The modern era began in 1796 when Edward Jenner developed the smallpox vaccine. This innovation paved the way for numerous vaccines against diseases like polio, measles, and influenza, significantly reducing mortality rates and transforming public health.

Understanding Inuit Ethnicity: Culture & Heritage

Inuit ethnicity refers to the indigenous peoples of the Arctic, primarily found in Canada, Alaska, and Greenland. Traditionally, they rely on subsistence hunting and fishing, showcasing rich cultural heritage through art, storytelling, and music. Their unique adaptations to the harsh environment highlight resilience and deep spiritual connections to the land.

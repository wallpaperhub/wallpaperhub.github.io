Kelp Forests: The Underwater Ecosystems You Need to Know

Kelp forests are underwater ecosystems formed by large kelp plants, primarily found in shallow coastal waters. These diverse habitats support countless marine species, including fish, invertebrates, and marine mammals. Kelp forests provide essential services, such as carbon absorption and habitat stability, making them vital for marine health.

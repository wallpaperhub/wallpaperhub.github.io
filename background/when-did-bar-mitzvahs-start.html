Understanding the History of Bar Mitzvahs

Bar mitzvahs, a significant rite of passage in Jewish tradition, date back to at least the 13th century. Initially marked by a formal blessing, the celebration evolved over centuries to include festive rituals and community gatherings. Today, it signifies a boy's coming of age at 13, highlighting his responsibilities within the Jewish faith.

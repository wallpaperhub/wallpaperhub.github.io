Understanding D-Day: The Normandy Invasion

D-Day refers to June 6, 1944, the day Allied forces launched the largest amphibious invasion in history on the beaches of Normandy, France, during World War II. This pivotal moment marked the beginning of the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi control. It was a monumental effort involving thousands of troops, aircraft, and naval vessels.

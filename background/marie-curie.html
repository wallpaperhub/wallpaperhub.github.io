Marie Curie: A Pioneer of Radioactivity

Marie Curie was a pioneering scientist known for her groundbreaking work in radioactivity, a term she coined. Born in Poland in 1867, she became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win in two different scientific fields: Physics and Chemistry. Her research laid the foundation for advances in medical treatments and nuclear physics.

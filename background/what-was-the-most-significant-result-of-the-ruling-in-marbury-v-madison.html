Key Outcome of Marbury v. Madison: Judicial Review Explained

The most significant result of the ruling in Marbury v. Madison (1803) was the establishment of the principle of judicial review. This landmark decision empowered the Supreme Court to nullify laws that conflicted with the Constitution, reinforcing the judiciary's role as a co-equal branch of government and ensuring a check on legislative and executive powers.

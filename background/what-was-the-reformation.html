The Reformation: A Turning Point in Christianity

The Reformation was a 16th-century religious movement that sought to reform the Catholic Church, leading to the rise of Protestantism. Key figures like Martin Luther challenged church practices, emphasizing faith and scripture's authority. This pivotal transformation reshaped Christianity, politics, and society across Europe.

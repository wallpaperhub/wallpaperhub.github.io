Functions of Red Blood Cells: Essential Oxygen Transport

Red blood cells (RBCs) play a crucial role in the human body by transporting oxygen from the lungs to tissues and organs, and carrying carbon dioxide back to the lungs for exhalation. Their unique biconcave shape increases surface area for gas exchange, while hemoglobin within them binds oxygen, ensuring efficient delivery of vital supplies to cells.

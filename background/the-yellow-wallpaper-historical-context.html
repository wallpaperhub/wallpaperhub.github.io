"Understanding the Historical Context of The Yellow Wallpaper"

"The Yellow Wallpaper," written by Charlotte Perkins Gilman in 1892, reflects the societal attitudes toward women's mental health during the late 19th century. It captures the struggles against the oppressive structures of patriarchy and the rest cure prescribed for women. This seminal work critiques the lack of autonomy faced by women in a male-dominated society, making it a poignant commentary on gender and mental illness.

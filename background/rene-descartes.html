Understanding René Descartes: The Father of Modern Philosophy

René Descartes, a French philosopher and mathematician, is often hailed as the father of modern philosophy. His method of doubt and emphasis on rationalism laid the groundwork for Enlightenment thinking. Descartes is famous for his statement "Cogito, ergo sum" (I think, therefore I am), which highlights the importance of self-awareness in existence.

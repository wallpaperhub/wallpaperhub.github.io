What "Peaky Blinders" Really Means: A Deep Dive

"Peaky Blinders" refers to a notorious gang in Birmingham, England, active in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The name is derived from the habit of sewing razor blades into the peaks of their caps, which they used as weapons. The show, centered around the Shelby family, explores themes of power, crime, and resilience in post-war Britain.

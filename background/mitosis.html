Mitosis Explained: The Cell Division Process

Mitosis is the process by which a single cell divides to produce two identical daughter cells, essential for growth, repair, and reproduction in multicellular organisms. It includes phases: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase, each ensuring that genetic material is evenly distributed, maintaining genetic continuity and stability.

Big Brother: Surveillance and Society Today

Big Brother is an omnipresent figure that symbolizes surveillance, control, and authority. Originating from George Orwell's dystopian novel "1984," it represents the dangers of totalitarianism and the loss of personal freedoms. In today's digital age, the concept resonates with debates on privacy and government oversight, making it more relevant than ever.

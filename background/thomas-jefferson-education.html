Thomas Jefferson's Vision for Education

Thomas Jefferson, an influential Founding Father and the third President of the United States, was a fervent advocate of education. He believed in cultivating an informed citizenry, emphasizing the importance of knowledge in democracy. Jefferson founded the University of Virginia, promoting public education and enlightenment ideals, which he saw as essential for national progress.

Understanding Background Fluorescence: A Key in Microscopy

Background fluorescence refers to the unwanted light emitted by a sample or surrounding materials when exposed to a specific wavelength of excitatory light. This phenomenon can obscure the detection of weak signals in fluorescence microscopy and spectroscopy, leading to challenges in data interpretation. Understanding and minimizing background fluorescence is crucial for accurate results in scientific research.

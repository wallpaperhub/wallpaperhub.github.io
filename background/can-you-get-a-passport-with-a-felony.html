Getting a Passport with a Felony: What You Need to Know

Yes, you can obtain a passport with a felony, provided you're not currently incarcerated for a felony conviction. The U.S. Department of State generally does not deny passport applications based solely on criminal records. However, certain conditions, such as outstanding warrants or parole limitations, could affect eligibility.

How Denver Got Its Name: The Story Behind the City

Denver was named after Kansas Territory Governor James W. Denver in 1858. The city's founders wished to attract settlers and businesses to the area. Initially a mining town, Denver quickly developed into a commercial hub. Its name symbolizes both ambition and growth during the Gold Rush era, making it a pivotal location in the West.

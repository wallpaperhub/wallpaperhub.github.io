Empire State Building Construction: A Marvel of Engineering

The Empire State Building, an American cultural icon, was constructed between 1930 and 1931 in New York City. Designed by Shreve, Lamb & Harmon, it stands at 1,454 feet tall, featuring 102 stories. The construction employed 3,400 workers, who completed it in just over a year, showcasing remarkable engineering and Art Deco design.

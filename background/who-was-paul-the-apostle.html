Who Was Paul the Apostle? A Key Figure in Early Christianity

Paul the Apostle, originally known as Saul of Tarsus, was a key figure in early Christianity. A former persecutor of Christians, he converted after an encounter with Jesus on the road to Damascus. Paul became an influential missionary, spreading the gospel across the Roman Empire and authoring several New Testament letters that shape Christian theology.

Understanding Black History Month Colors

Black History Month colors symbolize resilience, strength, and hope. The primary colors—red, black, and green—represent the struggles and achievements of the African American community. Red stands for the bloodshed in the fight for freedom, black represents the people, and green symbolizes the lushness of the land and prosperity.

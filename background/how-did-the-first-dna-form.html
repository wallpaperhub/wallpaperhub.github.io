How the First DNA Formed: Origins of Life Explained

The first DNA likely formed through a complex interplay of simple molecules in primordial environments, around 3.5 billion years ago. Researchers postulate that ribonucleic acids (RNA) provided a scaffold for early DNA, facilitated by environmental catalysts. This evolution led to the remarkable self-replicating structures that underpin all life today.

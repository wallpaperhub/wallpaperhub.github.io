Korematsu v. United States: A Landmark Case on Civil Liberties

Korematsu v. United States was a landmark Supreme Court case in 1944 that upheld the internment of Japanese Americans during World War II. Fred Korematsu's legal challenge argued this policy violated constitutional rights. The decision was controversial and has since been viewed as a grave injustice, highlighting racial discrimination and civil liberties issues.

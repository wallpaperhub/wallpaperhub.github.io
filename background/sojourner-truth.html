Sojourner Truth: Trailblazer for Freedom and Equality

Sojourner Truth was a former enslaved woman who became a prominent abolitionist and women's rights activist in the 19th century. Born in New York in 1797, she is best known for her powerful speech, "Ain't I a Woman?", which addressed both gender and racial inequalities. Her life exemplified resilience and the fight for justice.

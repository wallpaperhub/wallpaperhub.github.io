Bridget Bishop: The First Victim of the Salem Witch Trials

Bridget Bishop was one of the first individuals executed during the Salem witch trials in 1692. A tavern owner, her trial was marked by accusations of witchcraft based on her assertive demeanor and unorthodox behavior. Despite a lack of substantial evidence, she was convicted and hanged, exemplifying the hysteria of the era.

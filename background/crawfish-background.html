Understanding Crawfish: Origin & Culinary Delights

Crawfish, also known as crayfish or crawdads, are freshwater crustaceans found in North America, especially in Louisiana. They thrive in rivers, swamps, and lakes. Known for their distinctive pincers and flavorful meat, crawfish are often a staple in Southern cuisine, featuring prominently in dishes like gumbo and boils, especially during spring festivals.

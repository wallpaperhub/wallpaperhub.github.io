History of Yom Kippur: Significance & Traditions

Yom Kippur, known as the Day of Atonement, is the holiest day in Judaism, observed since ancient times. It occurs on the 10th day of Tishrei and emphasizes repentance, reflection, and prayer. Traditionally, fasting and synagogue services mark the day, allowing individuals to seek forgiveness for their sins and strive for spiritual renewal.

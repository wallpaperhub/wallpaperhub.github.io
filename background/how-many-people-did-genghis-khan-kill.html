Genghis Khan: Estimating the Death Toll of His Conquests

Genghis Khan, founder of the Mongol Empire, is estimated to have caused the deaths of approximately 40 million people during his conquests in the 13th century. This staggering figure accounts for nearly 10% of the world's population at that time. His military campaigns were marked by extreme violence and destruction, reshaping vast territories.

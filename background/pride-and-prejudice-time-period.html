"Exploring the Regency Era in Pride and Prejudice"

"Pride and Prejudice," written by Jane Austen in the early 19th century, reflects the societal norms and class distinctions of the Regency Era (1811-1820). This period was marked by strict social hierarchies, gender roles, and romantic entanglements. Through its lively characters, the novel critiques the culture of wealth and marriage, highlighting the struggles and aspirations of women in a male-dominated society.

Who Invented Graffiti? The Origins of Street Art

Graffiti, as a form of expression, dates back to ancient civilizations, notably the Romans and Greeks. However, modern graffiti emerged in the late 1960s in New York City, where artists like Cornbread and TAKI 183 innovated the art of tagging. Today, it encompasses diverse styles and messages, reflecting urban culture and social issues.

How Al Capone Met His End

Al Capone, the infamous mobster, died on January 25, 1947, from complications of syphilis and pneumonia. After serving time for tax evasion, his health deteriorated significantly. He spent his final years in a Florida mansion, suffering from mental and physical decline, before passing away at the age of 48.

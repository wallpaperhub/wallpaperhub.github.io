Why the Great Wall of China Was Built: History Unveiled

The Great Wall of China was built primarily to protect the Chinese states from invasions and raids by nomadic groups from the north. It also served as a means of border control, regulating trade along the Silk Road, and solidifying the presence of the ruling dynasties. Over centuries, it evolved into a symbol of strength and unity.

Exploring the Origins and Evolution of Haiku

Haiku is a traditional Japanese poetic form that emerged in the 17th century as the hokku, the opening stanza of a linked-verse poem. It became independent in the 19th century, emphasizing nature, seasons, and a momentary experience. Its 17 syllables, structured in a 5-7-5 pattern, invite simplicity and depth in expression.

Who Owns Tahiti? Understanding Its Governance

Tahiti, the largest island in French Polynesia, is an overseas collectivity of France. It is part of the larger Society Islands archipelago and is known for its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and tourism. The island, while retaining its Polynesian heritage, is administered as part of France, intertwining local traditions with French influence.

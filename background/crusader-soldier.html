Crusader Soldiers: Knights of the Holy Wars

Crusader soldiers were medieval warriors who took part in the Crusades, a series of religious wars initiated by the Catholic Church during the late 11th to the 13th centuries. These knights, driven by faith and the promise of salvation, traveled to the Holy Land to reclaim Jerusalem from Muslim control, facing battles, hardships, and cultural exchanges along the way.

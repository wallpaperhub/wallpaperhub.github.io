Understanding Background Extinction: Nature's Ongoing Process

Background extinction refers to the normal, ongoing rate of species extinction that occurs due to natural processes, rather than drastic events. This slow, gradual loss of biodiversity is a part of Earth's evolutionary history, shaped by factors such as climate change, habitat loss, and ecological relationships, resulting in the natural turnover of species over time.

Jacob Riis: Exposing Urban Poverty and Social Reform

Jacob Riis was a pioneering photojournalist and social reformer in the late 19th century, known for his work exposing the harsh conditions of the urban poor in New York City. His landmark book, "How the Other Half Lives," combined powerful photographs and narratives, influencing public awareness and leading to significant reforms in housing and labor laws.

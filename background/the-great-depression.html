Understanding the Great Depression: Causes & Effects

The Great Depression, which lasted from 1929 to the late 1930s, was a severe worldwide economic downturn. Triggered by the stock market crash of 1929, it led to massive unemployment, widespread poverty, and significant decline in industrial production. Governments implemented reforms, ultimately paving the way for recovery during World War II.

Understanding BSD: A Brief Overview

BSD (Berkeley Software Distribution) is a Unix-like operating system derived from the original Unix. Developed at the University of California, Berkeley, BSD features advanced networking and security capabilities. It served as the foundation for many modern operating systems, including FreeBSD and OpenBSD, emphasizing stability and performance.

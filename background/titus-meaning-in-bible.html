The Meaning of Titus in the Bible Explained

Titus is a significant figure in the New Testament, known as a companion of Paul and a trusted leader in the early Christian church. The name Titus means "title of honor" or "defender." He is best known for his work in Crete and the Epistle of Titus, which emphasizes sound doctrine and good deeds among believers.

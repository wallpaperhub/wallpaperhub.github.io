Loving v. Virginia: A Landmark Case for Interracial Marriage

"Loving v. Virginia" is a landmark Supreme Court case that legalized interracial marriage in the United States. Decided in 1967, it challenged laws prohibiting such unions. The case stemmed from the marriage of Richard and Mildred Loving and highlighted the conflict between personal freedom and state laws, paving the way for equality.

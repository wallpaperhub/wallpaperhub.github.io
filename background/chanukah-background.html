Explore the Rich History of Chanukah: The Festival of Lights

Chanukah, also known as the Festival of Lights, commemorates the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem after the Maccabean Revolt against the Seleucid Empire in 165 BCE. It is celebrated for eight nights, starting on the 25th of Kislev, with traditions including lighting the menorah, spinning dreidels, and enjoying fried foods.

Understanding the Bill of Rights: Essential Freedoms Explained

The Bill of Rights, ratified in 1791, comprises the first ten amendments to the United States Constitution. It guarantees essential rights and liberties such as freedom of speech, religion, and press, as well as the right to bear arms and protections against unreasonable searches. These fundamental rights shape American democracy and protect citizens from government overreach.

Auguste Comte: The Father of Sociology Explained

Auguste Comte, the father of sociology, pioneered positivism, emphasizing empirical evidence and scientific methods to understand society. He introduced the concept of "social physics" and outlined the stages of societal development. His work laid the groundwork for future sociological research, shaping modern social theories and practices.

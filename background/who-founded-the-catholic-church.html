Founding the Catholic Church: The Role of Jesus and Peter

The Catholic Church was founded by Jesus Christ, who appointed the Apostle Peter as its first leader. This establishment began around 30 AD, rooted in Jesus' teachings and his call for his followers to spread the Gospel. Over centuries, the Church evolved, shaping into a significant institution in Christianity worldwide.

Understanding the Big Bang Theory: The Origin of the Universe

The Big Bang Theory is the leading explanation for the origin of the universe, positing that it began as an infinitely small, hot, and dense point approximately 13.8 billion years ago. It expanded rapidly, cooling over time, leading to the formation of galaxies, stars, and planets. Observable cosmic background radiation supports this theory.

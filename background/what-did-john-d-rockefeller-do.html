What Did John D. Rockefeller Accomplish?

John D. Rockefeller was a pioneering American industrialist and philanthropist who co-founded the Standard Oil Company in 1870. He revolutionized the oil industry and defined the structure of modern philanthropy by donating vast sums to education, health, and scientific research, establishing institutions like the Rockefeller Foundation.

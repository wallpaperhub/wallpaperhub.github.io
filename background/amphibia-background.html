Exploring the Fascinating World of Amphibians

Amphibians, belonging to the class Amphibia, are remarkable creatures that transition between water and land. With moist skin and a life cycle often involving aquatic larvae, they thrive in diverse environments. This group includes frogs, toads, salamanders, and caecilians, showcasing a vast array of adaptations and behaviors. Their ecological importance and sensitivity to environmental changes make them vital indicators of ecosystem health.

Understanding Background Extinction Rates: A Critical Insight

The rate of background extinctions per year is estimated to be about 1 to 5 species per million species. This rate is a natural occurrence, but current human activities have accelerated extinction rates, leading to a biodiversity crisis. Conservation efforts are vital to protect endangered species and maintain ecological balance.
